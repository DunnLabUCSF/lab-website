Open Positions

Graduate Students

We are seeking passionate PhD candidates who can see themselves (pun intended) advancing retina research. Our lab offers:

  • State-of-the-art microscopes that cost more than your undergraduate education
  • A coffee machine that's probably smarter than our AI algorithms
  • Mentorship from experts who still remember their grad school trauma
  • Travel to conferences where you'll pretend to understand everyone's posters
  • Access to UCSF's facilities, including our legendary midnight snack drawer

Professional Requirements:

  • BSc in Neuroscience, Biology, or "I took a neuroscience class once and loved it"
  • Ability to spot a single fluorescent cell among millions (like finding your keys in a messy room)
  • Python coding skills beyond "Hello World" and "print('Help me')"
  • Experience with microscopes (Instagram filters don't count)
  • Statistical knowledge that exceeds calculating restaurant tips

Personal Characteristics:

  • Can survive on questionable amounts of caffeine
  • Immune to the existential crisis of failed experiments
  • Expert at explaining to family why you're still in school
  • Capable of making eye puns without making everyone groan
  • Commitment to science that outlasts your smartphone's battery life

Postdoctoral Fellows

Seeking brilliant minds who've survived their PhD and are ready for more! We offer:

  • Freedom to pursue your wildest scientific hypotheses (within reason and budget)
  • Salary that finally lets you eat something besides ramen
  • Career guidance from people who somehow made it in academia
  • Access to cutting-edge equipment (that you'll have to share, sorry)
  • Opportunities to collaborate with clinicians who speak a completely different language
  • Grant writing experience (because who doesn't love that?)

Professional Requirements:

  • PhD in Vision Science or related field (interpretive dance thesis not accepted)
  • Publication record more impressive than your Netflix watching record
  • Expert in retina research, specifically:
  • - Can name at least 5 retinal cell types without Googling
  • - Knows why the retina is actually brain tissue (and enjoys explaining it)
  • - Can operate complex imaging equipment without breaking it
  • Mastery of at least three programming languages (HTML doesn't count)
  • Grant writing skills that would impress Shakespeare

Personal Characteristics:

  • Maintains optimism despite reviewer comments
  • Can mentor students without flashbacks to your own PhD
  • Ability to explain your research to both Nobel laureates and your grandparents
  • Diplomatic skills for handling shared equipment scheduling conflicts
  • Zen-like patience for Western blots that refuse to work

* Must have a genuine interest in vision science (watching "The Matrix" doesn't qualify)

How to Apply

Interested candidates should send the following materials to retina.lab@ucsf.edu:

  • Detailed CV with complete publication list
  • Research statement (2 pages max) highlighting:
  • - Your previous research experience in retina/vision science
  • - Your technical expertise relevant to our research
  • - Your research interests and career goals
  • Contact information for three academic references
  • Cover letter explaining your interest in our lab and how your expertise aligns with our research
  • PDFs of your key publications (if applicable)

* We particularly encourage applications from candidates with experience in retinal development, disease modeling, or advanced imaging techniques. UCSF is an Equal Opportunity/Affirmative Action Employer.

Our Lab Culture

We foster an inclusive, collaborative, and innovative research environment. Our team is committed to advancing scientific knowledge while supporting individual growth and work-life balance.
