✨ Principal Investigator 👁️

🦋

Felice Dunn, PhD

Professor

Felice completed her Sc.B. and A.B. at Brown University, with honors theses in Neuroscience and Visual Arts. She earned her Ph.D. from the University of Washington and completed postdoctoral fellowships at the National Institutes of Health and University of Washington before establishing her lab at UCSF.

✨ Postdoctoral Fellows 👀

🐰

Joo Yeun Lee

Postdoctoral Fellow

JL completed her graduate work at USC, where she studied synaptic plasticity in Drosophila neuromuscular junction. Besides being a neuroscientist, she is passionate about artistic creativity and fascinated with communicating science using visual art.

🐼

Annika Balraj

Postdoctoral Fellow

Annika completed her dissertation work at George Washington University, studying the impact of demyelination in optic nerve conduction and retinal organization. Her current project focuses on tuning properties of direction-selective retinal ganglion cells after cone loss. In her free time, she enjoys going to museums, reading, hiking, and petting friendly dogs.

🦊

Paige Leary

Postdoctoral Fellow

Paige completed her graduate work in David Schoppik's lab at NYU, where she studied how early development shapes neural circuit function using the larval zebrafish gaze stabilization system. Her current work in the Dunn lab studies cone circuit changes following graded rod loss. Outside of lab, she enjoys exploring the city, making coffee, and playing cello.

✨ Lab Manager 👁️

🐢

Thomas Ta

Lab Manager / Research Specialist

Thomas was born and raised in San Francisco and graduated from UCLA with a BS in Biology. He completed the CCSF Biotechnology program and earned two UCSF research internships. As a research specialist in the Dunn Lab, he hopes to shed light on the molecular mechanisms of the optokinetic reflex (OKR) and its significance in retinal disease. In his free time, Thomas enjoys spending time with his family, trying to fix random things, and sight-seeing.

✨ Graduate Student 👀

🦉

Jeffrey Yang

Neuroscience Program Graduate Student

Jeffrey found his interest in synaptic plasticity and neuroscience during his time at UCLA researching under Dr. Dean Buonomano and Dr. Leanna Hernandez. He is very excited to research how plasticity can differentially shape RGC function and computations. In his free time, he enjoys patching cells (when they are kind to him), cooking, and spending time with his partner and cat!
